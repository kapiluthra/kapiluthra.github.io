Who I Am
  • I've spent 13 years in the trenches of enterprise content management — from junior engineer to solutions architect, across three continents and some of the world's most complex IT environments.
  • I'm deeply passionate about the intersection of AI and enterprise infrastructure. Most people building RAG pipelines don't understand ECM. Most ECM architects don't understand RAG. I'm trying to be the bridge.
  • I believe in building in public — writing about what I learn, publishing the messy parts, and treating every project as a chance to teach someone something useful.
  • I have a strong bias towards ownership. When a migration goes sideways at 2am, I'm the one on the call. That instinct shaped how I architect — defensively, with fallbacks, and with clear runbooks.
  • I moved to Denmark for the work and stayed for the quality of life. The flat hierarchy and direct communication style suits how I work.
  • When I'm not in a terminal or on a client call, I'm probably exploring something new in the AI/ML space — currently obsessed with reranking, retrieval evaluation, and agentic architectures for enterprise use cases.

Philosophy

"I specialize in making enterprise content findable, queryable, and trustworthy at scale."

  • Good architecture is mostly about knowing where to draw boundaries — what to abstract, what to couple, and what to leave explicit. The projects that age well are the ones with clear ownership and boring technology choices.
  • The best migrations I've run are the ones where we verified everything twice and moved fast as a result. The worst ones skipped the preparation and paid for it in production at 2am.
  • Writing forces precision. If I can't explain an architectural decision in plain text, I probably don't understand it well enough. That's why this blog exists.
  • I'm more interested in what RAG gets wrong than what it gets right — hallucination modes, retrieval failures, ACL gaps. Enterprise deployments fail at the edges, not the happy path.
